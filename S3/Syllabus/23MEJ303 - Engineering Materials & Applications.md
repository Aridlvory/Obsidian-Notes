# MODULE I (7 hours)
- [ ] Metals, polymers, ceramics and composites; Relevant properties (physical, mechanical, thermal, electrical, chemical)
- [ ] Cost; Range of applications;
- [ ] Mechanical properties & Testing: Young’s modulus; Tensile Testing,
- [ ] Yielding and yield strength; ductility, resilience, toughness
- [ ] Compression test, Fatigue Test, SN curve, endurance limits
- [ ] Atomic bonding – types, Crystallography, BC, BCC, FCC unit cells
- [ ] APF & Theoretical Density calculations, Miller indices,
- [ ] Crystalline defects – brief review Self-Study
# MODULE II (5 hours)
- [ ] Metals & Alloys: Iron and steel alloys; Stainless steel;
- [ ] Aluminum Alloys; Titanium alloys
- [ ] Phase diagrams: Lever rule, simple problems, Iron Iron-carbide phase diagram
- [ ] Development of microstructure in iron-carbon alloys, Invariant Reactions, Pearlite, Bainite, Martensite
- [ ] Phase transformation, Isothermal transformation diagram, TTT and CCT diagrams, applications
# MODULE III (4 hours)
- [ ] Heat treatment of steel: Annealing, normalizing,
- [ ] Tempering, Austempering, Martempering
- [ ] Case hardening: Carburizing, nitriding, cyaniding, carbo-nitriding
- [ ] Flame and induction hardening, vacuum and plasma hardening
# MODULE IV (4 hours)
- [ ] Polymers – Classification and applications; Polymerization techniques
- [ ] Ceramics – Classifications and applications
- [ ] Bio-ceramics and Glasses; Self-Study
- [ ] Composites – Reinforcement, matrix, metal matrix composites, ceramic matrix composites, polymer composites
- [ ] Other advanced materials – biomaterials
- [ ] Optical materials Self-Study
# MODULE V (4 hours)
- [ ] Conducting and resisting materials – types, properties and applications;
- [ ] Semiconducting materials – properties and applications Self-Study
- [ ] Magnetic materials – Soft and hard magnetic materials and applications;
- [ ] Smart materials; Sensors and actuators; 
- [ ] Piezoelectric materials Self-Study
- [ ] Magnetostrictive and electro strictive materials.
