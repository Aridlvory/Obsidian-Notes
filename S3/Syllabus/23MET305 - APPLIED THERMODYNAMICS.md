# MODULE 1 Review of Thermodynamics (9 Hours)
- [ ] **Introduction and Review of Thermodynamics** - Definition of Thermodynamics, First Law of Thermodynamics, Limitations of First Law of Thermodynamics and Introduction to, Second Law, Engineering Applications of Steady Flow Energy Equation
- [ ] **Performance analysis of vapor power cycle.** - Introduction to Steam engineering., Rankine Cycle
- [ ] Modified Rankine Cycle
- [ ] **Improvement in Rankine cycles** - Reheat Cycle, Regenerative Cycle (Self Study)
- [ ] Binary Vapor Cycle
- [ ] **Performance analysis of Gas power cycles**: Brayton cycle: Intercooling
- [ ] Reheat cycle, Regenerative cycle (self-study)
- [ ] **Performance analysis of Air standard cycle** - Constant Volume or Otto Cycle (Air standard efficiency, No derivation}
- [ ] Constant Pressure or Diesel Cycle (Air standard efficiency, No derivation}, Actual IC engine cycle

# MODULE II Steam turbines (6 Hours)
- [ ] **Flow of steam through nozzle** - Important Terms Relating to Steam Formation, Properties of Steam
- [ ] Thermodynamic Properties of Steam and Steam Tables
- [ ] **Steam turbines** - Working Principle of a Steam Turbine, Classification of Steam Turbines
- [ ] Impulse and reaction turbine, work done, efficiency , Condition for maximum efficiency (Self-study), stage efficiency (No derivation)
- [ ] Steam turbine performance-reheat factor, degree of reaction. Governing of turbines. (Self-study).
- [ ] Compounding of turbines-pressure velocity variation

# MODULE III Compressors (6 Hours)
- [ ] **Single stage Reciprocating compressor** - Classification of compressors. Reciprocating compressor-single stage compressor,
- [ ] Equation for work with and without clearance volume, efficiencies (No derivations) Limitations of Single-Stage Compression
- [ ] **Multistage compressor** - Intercooler. Free air delivered (FAD).
- [ ] Multistage compressor with Intercooler.
- [ ] **Centrifugal compressor** - Working, velocity diagram,
- [ ] Work done, power required, isentropic efficiency, slip factor. Surging and chocking, Axial compressors.

# MODULE IV compressible flow (6 Hours)
- [ ] **Compressible Flow** - Introduction to compressible fluid flow.
- [ ] Basic Equations of Compressible Fluid
- [ ] Use of ideal gas tables for isentropic flow and normal shock flow
- [ ] Compressible flow in diffusers. Efficiency of nozzle and diffuser
- [ ] **Types of flows** - Stagnation properties. Isentropic flow of a perfect gas through a nozzle.
- [ ] Choked flow, Subsonic and supersonic flows- normal shocks

# MODULE V Combustion (8 Hours)
- [ ] Introduction to solid, liquid, and gaseous fuels. Stoichiometry.
- [ ] Combustion equations of solid and gaseous fuels.
- [ ] Theoretical or minimum mass of air required for complete combustion,
- [ ] Theoretical or minimum volume of air required for complete combustion,
- [ ] Conversion of Volumetric analysis in to mass analysis or gravimetric analysis
- [ ] Conversion of mass analysis in to Volumetric analysis
- [ ] Determination of Mass of carbon in flue gases,
- [ ] Determination of mass of flue gases per kg of fuel burnt.
